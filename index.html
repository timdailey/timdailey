I assess where you stand today and build a roadmap to improve it.

About

20 Years. A Few Industries.
One Through-Line.

I started in web development and information architecture before "digital marketing" was a widely used phrase. Since then I've watched the industry evolve through every major shift — from early SEO to social, from content marketing to marketing automation, and now into the AI-driven search era. I've stayed close to the technical side while building the strategic perspective that only comes from doing this work across very different kinds of organizations.

My career has spanned healthcare, financial services, and B2B SaaS. At ForHealth Consulting (part of UMass Medical School), I spent over a decade building web strategy for public health initiatives — work that demanded rigor, clarity, and the ability to reach audiences that weren't already looking for you. At Citizens Bank, I led website strategy at a scale where every UX decision carried real consequence. At EDB and Aerospike, I served as product owner for complex marketing websites — leading redesigns, platform migrations, and the transition into GEO and AI search optimization. Most recently, I consulted with GridGain on a full-site engagement that included glossary content reaching position one in Google and appearing in AI Overview results.

What I've learned across all of it: the fundamentals work. Understand your audience. Make it easy for them to find you, understand you, and trust you. Then optimize relentlessly.

I'm based in Groton, Massachusetts, and work with clients across the U.S. I'm selective about the engagements I take on — not because I'm unavailable, but because I do my best work when I'm genuinely invested in what a client is building.

Before the director titles and the enterprise clients, there was a lot of hands-on work that built the foundation for all of it.

In the late 1990s and early 2000s, I was designing and developing websites when most businesses didn't have one yet — building multimedia CD-ROMs and early web experiences at HealthStream and SilverPlatter Education, then moving into visual design and UI work at Show and Tell Inc. Those years taught me to think about usability before there was much of a vocabulary for it.

From roughly 2001 through 2010, alongside my other work, I freelanced for small businesses and several school districts — building and maintaining websites, helping organizations figure out what their digital presence should even be. Many of these clients had never worked with a web professional before, which meant translating technical decisions into plain language was part of the job from the start. That experience is a big reason I'm just as comfortable working with a solo financial advisor as I am with a Series B software company.

At Needham High School, I wore several different hats over the years — district webmaster, technology support, and briefly, teacher. I taught Photography and Graphic Design, secured a grant to bring digital photography into the curriculum, and ran adult education courses in web design. Teaching turned out to be useful training for consulting: you get good at explaining things clearly, meeting people where they are, and making complicated subjects feel manageable.

My very first professional web role was at Curry College, where I developed and maintained the college's first-ever website while managing their Educational Technology Services Office. It's a long way from there to leading a platform migration to Contentful and React — but the thread connecting all of it is the same: figure out what someone needs from a digital experience, then build it as well as you possibly can.

Work

A Few Things I've Worked On

I don't believe in long case study PDFs that nobody reads. Here are the situations, the approaches, and what came out the other side.

SEO, GEO & Full-Site Optimization — Database Technology

∨
The situation

A B2B database technology company needed its website to work harder across SEO, user experience, and AI search visibility. Landing pages, product pages, and the blog had accumulated technical debt, and the site's information architecture wasn't guiding visitors toward conversion.

The approach

I rebuilt and optimized landing pages, the homepage, and product pages for both search and UX. I developed a targeted keyword and GEO strategy aligned to technical buyer journeys, reworked the site navigation and information architecture, and built out a library of glossary pages designed to capture bottom-of-funnel search intent. I also resolved technical SEO issues holding back the trials and downloads experience.

What happened

Several glossary pages reached position one in Google search results. Multiple pages also began appearing in Google's AI Overview results — organic visibility extending into the AI-driven layer of search that most competitors hadn't begun to address yet.

Scaling a Public Health Web Presence — Academic Medical Institution

∨
The situation

ForHealth Consulting (University of Massachusetts Medical School) operated a portfolio of public health programs, each with its own web presence, audience, and compliance requirements. There was no unified strategy, no shared standards, and significant redundancy.

The approach

Over 12 years, I built the web strategy infrastructure that brought those properties into alignment — from content governance frameworks and UX standards to analytics implementation and CMS migration work. I managed day-to-day execution for multiple concurrent sites simultaneously.

What happened

The organization gained a coherent digital presence across programs, with measurable improvements in user engagement and a significant reduction in the time it took to launch new initiatives. The work also built internal capacity where there had been almost none.

Website Strategy at Scale — Financial Services

∨
The situation

Citizens Bank needed to tighten the connection between its digital experience and measurable business outcomes — a complex challenge with significant regulatory constraints, massive content volume, and multiple stakeholder groups with competing priorities.

The approach

I led website strategy efforts focused on the intersection of UX, SEO, and conversion — identifying where the experience was breaking down and building the internal alignment needed to fix it. This meant bringing together product, compliance, marketing, and technology stakeholders around a shared prioritization framework.

What happened

Page-level performance improved across key acquisition flows. Equally valuable: the organization developed a cleaner process for making website decisions — one that was faster, better-documented, and less dependent on institutional memory.

AI Search Visibility Audit — B2B SaaS Platform

∨
The situation

A SaaS company in a competitive data infrastructure category noticed that AI assistants were either not mentioning their product in relevant conversations or describing it in ways that were incomplete and occasionally inaccurate.

The approach

I ran a structured AI visibility assessment across multiple dimensions — content openness, structured data, brand authority signals, messaging clarity, and intent signal infrastructure. The audit surfaced both quick wins and longer-horizon strategic priorities.

What happened

The team had a clear, prioritized roadmap for improving their AI discoverability — and a framework for measuring progress over time. Several high-impact changes were implemented within 60 days of the engagement.

SEO & AI Visibility — Independent Financial Advisory Practice

∨
The situation

A solo financial advisor had a professional website but no real search presence — invisible in local search results and absent from AI-driven recommendations, even for queries their ideal clients were clearly asking.

The approach

I ran a combined SEO and AI visibility audit, identifying gaps in local search optimization, schema markup, and the type of authoritative content that helps practices appear in AI-generated recommendations. The fixes didn't require a massive content investment — just the right ones, done correctly.

What happened

The practice began appearing in local search results for relevant queries within weeks, and AI tools started surfacing them more accurately when asked about financial advisors in their area. The advisor had a clear picture of what was working and a simple ongoing checklist to maintain it.
